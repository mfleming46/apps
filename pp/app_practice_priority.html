What is Practice Priority?

This app keeps your practice items in order using a system similar to Dr. Molly Gebrian's red-yellow-green method.

  1. Divide your music into sections based on difficulty:

    • Green = Grade 3 = Ready to perform
    • Yellow = Grade 2 = I could struggle through it
    • Red = Grade 1 = This is embarassing

  2. Practice red items first. After a few days, the reds will (hopefully) move up to yellows or greens.

  3. Items you don't practice lose 10% of their grade each day you don't practice them. That's the "aged grade". A green drop to a blue if you don't practice it for 4 days. It drops all the way to red in 11 days.

Keeping your practice list in aged-grade order focuses your practice on areas that need the most work, without totally ignoring the easier sections.

Basic Commands

Demo Data: Loads some sample data.

Clear Table: Remove all rows.

Add Row: Add a new practice item at the top. Don't forget to fill in the name of the item. Other columns will be filled automatically as you practice.

Delete Row: Delete the selected row

Practice: Start practicing. The app steps through the items one at a time, starting at the top.

Practice the selected item and click one of the grade buttons to assign a grade. The app records the date and grade then advances to the next item

If you prefer not to practice an item, click Skip to advance to the next one. To skip more than one item, click on a radio button in the Select column

When you complete the list the app stops and re-sorts the items by Aged Grade. You can also click Stop to quit before you have practiced all items.
